Politics // Washington DC

The 2026 US Midterms are dominated by the "Robot Tax" debate. As AI systems handle 45% of administrative labor, politicians are split on taxing these synthetic workers to fund a National Dividend. The outcome will decide the economic fate of the next fifty years and redefine the social contract between citizens and the state.

Algorithmic Governance

Beyond the tax debate, several cities are experimenting with "Liquid Democracy," where citizens can delegate their votes to trusted experts on a per-issue basis via secure blockchain apps. The move towards a more direct, technology-enabled form of representation is challenging the traditional role of the politician.
