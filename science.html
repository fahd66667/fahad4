Ares

Science // Gale Crater

The Ares-3 colony has successfully transmitted the first 8K live feed from the Martian surface. The footage shows the blue sunsets of Gale Crater and the massive hydroponic towers that now provide fresh food for the 400 resident scientists. This marks the beginning of true interplanetary settlement and the birth of the first Martian-born generation.

Biological Adaptation

Living on Mars has already begun to change the human body. Scientists are documenting shifts in bone density and cardiovascular efficiency in the pioneers. The goal is no longer just to survive, but to thrive in 38% gravity. This research is providing invaluable data that will eventually lead to the terraforming of the Red Planet.
