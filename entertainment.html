Immersive

Arts // Los Angeles

The "Flat-Film" era is over. The latest blockbuster, Neon Horizon, was watched by 150 million people who "inhabited" the film using VR headsets. Viewers can now choose their own POV, walking through movie sets while the actors perform around them in a 360-degree narrative experience.

The Death of the Script

With generative AI controlling background characters, no two viewings of the same film are exactly alike. The audience is no longer a passive observer; they are a presence within the world. This blurring of the line between gaming and cinema is creating a new medium that we are only just beginning to name.
